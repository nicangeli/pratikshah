  • Etail Distribution

    Consumer Electronics
    Etail Distribution was founded to help brands deal with the problem of excess inventory, providing a trusted disposition channel to a wide range of brands in the consumer electronics sector.
     
    Dealing with excess inventory can be a huge challenge, given the number of commercial factors and stakeholders that need to be taken into account by interested parties. Over the past 5 years, we've worked with over 50 brands, helping to address this problem, bringing over half a million audio-visual products to market, a capability matched by few other disposition partners.
     
    Dealing with problem inventory has taught us a great deal about effective product marketing and brand building.  We therefore partner with emerging CE and accessory companies, working from our experience with excess inventory, to help build a successful European presence.

    Marketplace for medical tourism

    Elective is a project I started on Entrepreneur First. The basic premise was to build an online marketplace for medical tourism.

     

    Private healthcare costs have been spiralling out of control, a dental implant treatment can cost as much as 30,000, a huge out of pocket expense. Yet the same procedure may be available upto 70% less in another country, as I witnessed during my Asia trip.

     

    Regardless of the cost savings however, it is virtually impossible to distinguish between providers unless you know somebody who can provide a strong personal recommendation.

     

    The internet has enabled transparency in so many sectors, yet when it comes to healthcare, consumers lack power and choice, this is a huge problem.

     

    The idea behind Elective therefore is to facilitate trust and transparency in a globally fragmented marketplace, making it easy to find, compare and book affordable surgeries that patient's can trust, starting with dentistry.



  • Lawyering

    Warwick Law

    Law

    Before starting university I had the opportunity to work at a number of city firms, as well as spending time in chambers. It struck me as an incredibly stimulating profession, with the ability of analyse and advise on some highly complex commercial transactions.
     
    The study of law confirmed my curiosity - particularly with regard to contract law and its application to the commercial world. It was fascinating to see how the law could be used to achieve practical commercial ends. The development of retention of title clauses in Romalpa, and the innomate term in Hong Kong Fir are interesting cases in point.
     
    I also looked into areas of environmental law, during my undergraduate study - focusing on EU policy. I wrote two 5000 word dissertations,  one on the cap and trade system, the other on the common fishery policy. It struck me how badly the EU had regulated these areas, and how they had almost taken a completely free market approach to the management of these systems, leading to huge ineffiencies, not striking enough of a balance between hard law and flexible rules.

    My journey across Asia

    An incredible journey, backpacking solo across Thailand, Laos, Cambodia, Vietnam, Japan and China over 4 months - 118 days to be precise. 



    118 days across Asia

    In 2013 I concluded a four-month round trip of Asia, travelling through Thailand, Laos, Cambodia, Vietnam, Japan and China.
     
    It was the experience of a lifetime. I was able to immerse myself in numerous different cultures and gain a real insight into different ways of living.
     
    Constantly on the move and being consistently out of my comfort zone was one of the most exciting bits of the experience, especially as a solo traveller who was excited to overcome challenge.
     
    Living out of a backpack for 4 months showed how far you can go with to little, and how meaningless possessions can be.
     
    The best part of the experience was the ability to meet other travellers at every stage of the trip. The trip really would not have been the same without the hundreds of people I met along the way.

    I've now got friends in virtually every corner of the globe!



    San Francisco and the Bay Area Roadtrip

    Nick Angeli and I flew out to SF in May. We booked our trip with a few days to spare and were lucky to be able to take a 3 day roadtrip around the Bay Area.
     
    We started in SF and covered 500miles over the course of three days. In that time we covered Livermore, Mariposa, Yosemite, Gilroy, Santa Cruz, San Jose, Mountain View and Palo Alto.
     
    California is a beautiful state and my favourite place in the US.  It was amazing being able to drive through the open country roads around Yosemite.
     



    Canada and US Roadtrip

    In June, my sister and I embarked on an impromptu trip to Canada, meeting Amy and Ben from my Asia travels.
     
    We landed in Vancouver and drove to Calgary by road, driving through the Rocky Mountains and getting to experience some of the wonders of Banff National Park.
